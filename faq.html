Booking & Payments

Yes! We offer a 10% discount for groups of 4-6 people and a 15% discount for groups of 7 or more. Contact us for custom quotes for large groups or special events.

Travel insurance is not included in our tour prices. We strongly recommend purchasing comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical expenses, trip cancellation, and personal belongings.

Tour Details

What is included in your tour packages?

Our standard inclusions are: accommodation, all ground transport in private vehicle, services of an English-speaking guide, entrance fees to sites mentioned in itinerary, daily breakfast, and selected lunches. Specific inclusions vary by tour - see individual tour pages for details.

What is not included?

Typically not included: international flights, visa fees, travel insurance, dinners (unless specified), alcoholic beverages, personal expenses, tips for guides/drivers, and any activities not mentioned in the itinerary.

Can I customize a tour?

Absolutely! We specialize in creating custom itineraries. Tell us your interests, budget, and travel dates, and we'll design a personalized tour just for you. Custom tours typically require a bit more planning time.

We welcome solo travelers! For private tours, there is a single supplement charge. We can also help solo travelers join group tours when available, or connect you with other travelers for shared costs.

Travel Preparation

Do I need a visa for Sri Lanka?

Most nationalities require an Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) for tourist visits. You can apply online at eta.gov.lk. The standard tourist visa is valid for 30 days. We recommend applying at least 2 weeks before travel.

What vaccinations do I need?

Recommended vaccinations include Hepatitis A & B, typhoid, and routine vaccines. Depending on your travel plans, you may also consider rabies and Japanese encephalitis vaccines. Consult your doctor or a travel clinic 4-6 weeks before departure.

What should I pack?

Essentials include: lightweight clothing, comfortable walking shoes, swimwear, sunscreen, hat, insect repellent, adapter (UK-type plugs), and conservative clothing for temple visits (shoulders and knees covered). See our Packing List for details.

What is the dress code in Sri Lanka?

Sri Lanka is generally conservative. At beaches, swimwear is acceptable, but cover up when leaving the beach. For temples and religious sites, both men and women should cover shoulders and knees. Remove shoes and hats before entering temples.

Can I use my credit card in Sri Lanka?

Credit cards are widely accepted in cities and tourist areas. However, it's good to carry some Sri Lankan rupees for smaller purchases, markets, and rural areas. ATMs are available in most towns.

About Sri Lanka

What is the best time to visit Sri Lanka?

Sri Lanka has year-round appeal with two monsoon seasons affecting different regions. Generally, the west and south coasts are best from December to March, while the east coast is best from April to September. The hill country is pleasant year-round but cooler. See our Weather Guide for details.

Is Sri Lanka safe for tourists?

Sri Lanka is generally very safe for tourists. Violent crime against tourists is rare. Take normal precautions with valuables, be aware of your surroundings, and use registered taxis. We provide 24/7 support throughout your tour for added peace of mind.

What is the food like?

Sri Lankan cuisine is flavorful and diverse, featuring rice, curry, seafood, and tropical fruits. Food can be spicy, but mild options are available. There are also good international dining options in tourist areas. We're happy to accommodate dietary requirements.

What languages are spoken?

Sinhala and Tamil are the official languages, but English is widely spoken, especially in tourist areas, hotels, and by our guides. Road signs are in English, Sinhala, and sometimes Tamil.

What is the time zone?

Sri Lanka Standard Time (SLST) is UTC+5:30. There is no daylight saving time.

What is the voltage and plug type?

230V, 50Hz. Plug types D and G (British-style three rectangular pins). You'll need a travel adapter for most non-UK appliances.

Health & Safety

Is the tap water safe to drink?

It's recommended to drink bottled or purified water. Most hotels provide bottled water or have water purifiers. Avoid ice in drinks unless you're sure it's made from purified water.

What about mosquitoes and diseases?

Use insect repellent, especially at dusk. Dengue fever exists in Sri Lanka, but risk to tourists is generally low if you take precautions. Malaria risk is very low in most tourist areas. Consult your doctor about antimalarials if visiting rural areas.

What healthcare facilities are available?

Colombo has excellent private hospitals with international standards. Major cities have good medical facilities. We recommend comprehensive travel insurance that includes medical evacuation.

Are your vehicles and drivers safe?

Yes. All our vehicles are well-maintained, insured, and equipped with seat belts. Drivers are experienced, licensed, and follow defensive driving practices. We use modern, air-conditioned vehicles for comfort and safety.

Other Questions

Can I extend my stay?

Certainly! We can help arrange additional nights or extend your tour. Many travelers add beach time at the end of their cultural tour.

Do you offer airport transfers?

Yes, we can arrange airport pick-up and drop-off as part of your tour package. Transfers are in private vehicles with our drivers.

What if I have mobility issues?

Please inform us of any mobility concerns when booking. Some sites (like Sigiriya) involve significant climbing, but we can suggest alternative activities or adjusted itineraries.

How much should I tip?

Tipping is appreciated but not mandatory. Guidelines: guides $5-10 per day, drivers $3-5 per day, restaurants 10% if service charge not included. Tip according to your satisfaction with the service.

Can I get a SIM card for my phone?

Yes, SIM cards are easily available at the airport and in cities. You'll need to show your passport. Major providers are Dialog, Mobitel, and Hutch. Tourist packages with data are affordable.

What is your sustainable tourism policy?

We're committed to responsible tourism. We use locally-owned accommodations, employ local guides, support community projects, follow eco-friendly practices, and educate travelers on minimizing their environmental impact.
